Months 2–4 · The main gap; heavily weighted at all levels
Main gap ▼
Scalability Fundamentals

REST vs GraphQL, rate limiting, pagination, idempotency, service mesh, circuit breakers
System Design Primer (GitHub) — 250k+ stars, completely free, must-read
ByteByteGo YouTube — best free visual system design content; watch weekly
Designing Data-Intensive Applications (Kleppmann) — industry bible, read chapters 1–6 minimum
Grokking System Design — structured course with diagrams (paid, worth it)
Karan's System Design notes — free, clean, covers all the essentials
Target: Be able to design any of the 10 classic systems in 45 min. Lead with requirements clarification → capacity estimation → high-level design → deep dives → trade-offs. This is a practiced skill, not a knowledge test.

Core CS Breadth
Months 3–5 · What every big tech interview assumes you know
Build up ▼
Operating Systems
Processes vs threads, scheduling (FCFS, SJF, Round Robin), virtual memory, paging, deadlocks, semaphores, mutexes

OSI model, TCP vs UDP, HTTP/1.1 vs HTTP/2 vs HTTP/3, DNS resolution, TLS handshake, WebSockets, gRPC

B-tree & LSM-tree storage, WAL, MVCC, query planning, transactions, isolation levels (READ COMMITTED, SERIALIZABLE)
Concurrency
Race conditions, locks, atomic operations, thread pools, async/await, event loops, actor model
Beej's Guide to Network Programming — free, practical, excellent for networking depth
Operating Systems: Three Easy Pieces (OSTEP) — free online, the best OS fundamentals book
Deepen Go (great for concurrency interviews) or your existing C++ from competitive programming

Application Strategy
Sequencing your applications
Month 5: Apply to India unicorns first (Zepto, Flipkart, Swiggy) — use these as warm-up interviews to build confidence and get live reps.
Month 6: Apply to Microsoft and Amazon — strong brands, more predictable processes, good for building offer leverage.
Month 7: Apply to Google and Meta — save your peak readiness for the hardest processes. Having competing offers improves your negotiation position.
Always: Apply to 15–20 companies total, not just your top 3. Pipeline depth protects you from bad luck in any single process.
